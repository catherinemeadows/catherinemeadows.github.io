About

I am a Senior at George Washington University, majoring in Computer Science (BS) as well as minoring in Naval Science. I work as an Undergraduate Teaching Assistant for the Database Systems course and as a Research Assistant at GW.

My interests include networking, distributed systems, and real-time systems. When I'm not coding, I enjoy traveling and hiking! As a midshipman, I spend time working out and exploring the DC community.

Research & Projects

Undergraduate Research

I work with Dr. Timothy Wood's research group at GWU on networking and distributed systems research.

I've been fortunate to work with Dr. Wood during the school year as well as under the GWU Summer Undergraduate Engineering Research Fellowship (SUPER) Program (Summer 2020, Summer 2021)

  • Wrote network functions to process client packets in containerized network flows
  • Contribute to and serve as one of five Github maintainers for the high performance network function virtualization platform OpenNetVM (ONVM)
  • Work on load balancing with ONVM network functions for Serverless Edge environments
  • Attended Conferences: Grace Hopper Celebration (2021, 2020), IEEE Autonomic Computing & Self-Organizing Systems (2020)

Capstone Project

For my year-long Capstone Design course, my team and I created "Electri" - a system for crowdsourcing images from car dashcams and autonomous vehicle imaging systems for vehicle-based AMBER Alert reponse.

With advising from Dr. Timothy Wood, my team is developing:

  • AWS Lambda-based system with a stepflow of vehicle recognition, matching, and front end display of target vehicles in AMBER Alert cases
  • Cloud cost analysis of our proposed system
