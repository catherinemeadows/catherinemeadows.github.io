Electri

Crowdsourcing for AMBER Alert Response

Capstone Senior Design Project (August 2021 - May 2022)

George Washington University Computer Science

Our Project

AMBER Alerts are used by the U.S. Department of Justice to broadcast emergency messages and request help from the public about child abduction cases. According to the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children 2020 Report, approximately 82% of AMBER Alerts had vehicle involvement and 43% of AMBER Alert success stories resulted from recognizing a vehicle. Electri provides an opt-in service that sources images from dashboard cameras and autonomous vehicle cameras across the United States. Verified users (likely law enforcement or government authorities) can then view positively matched images of target vehicles, thus automating, maximizing, and streamlining data sourcing for vehicle-based AMBER Alerts.

Electri's mission is to leverage increasingly prevalent dashboard cameras and autonomous cars driving on the road today to maximize the successful resolution of AMBER Alerts. By automating responses to active AMBER Alerts, we not only provide a frictionless and easy-to-use service, but also drastically increase data sourcing capabilities with the sole purpose of saving lives as quickly and efficiently as possible.
